The Review That Actually Changes Something

A performance review that changes nothing was not a conversation; it was a rating ceremony. This guide turns the review from a backward-looking verdict into something that actually moves behaviour.

Fix the shape

  • Make it continuous: frequent short conversations, with the annual review as a summary.
  • Lead with conversation, not the number.
  • Look forward: spend most of it on what to build next.

Remove the surprises

  1. Give timely feedback all year, so the review confirms, never reveals.
  2. Anchor to specific moments, not vague traits.
  3. End with commitments, and revisit them before the next review.

Why it matters

People quickly learn whether the review is theatre or a real steering conversation, and they engage accordingly. Make it specific, timely, and forward-looking, and it stops being a ritual and starts being the thing that develops people.
