The Policy Comprehension Test

A policy nobody can restate is a policy that does not exist. Publishing is not communicating, and a signature is not understanding. This test checks whether a rule has actually landed, before an incident checks it for you.

The three-part test

  1. Restate: can a random person explain, in one sentence, what the policy asks of their job?
  2. Behave: is there a single observable behaviour the policy is meant to produce?
  3. Converge: given the same scenario, do different teams reach the same call?

Where policies break

Published, not communicated: access was mistaken for comprehension.
Signed, not understood: receipt was mistaken for behaviour change.
Abstract, not concrete: vague wording lets each team interpret its own way.

How to close the gap

  • Translate the rule into the specific behaviour it asks of each role.
  • Replace abstract wording with concrete examples of what to do and not do.
  • Embed the guidance where the decision happens, not only in the handbook.
  • Check for the behaviour on real work, not for the signature in the system.
