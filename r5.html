The Disambiguation Method: From Suitcase Word to Trainable Behaviour

You cannot close a gap you cannot name. This three-step method turns a fog like “communication” into a behaviour you can design for, measure, and prove.

The method

  1. Name the suitcase word. Whatever the brief says: communication, ownership, influence.
  2. Unpack it into observable behaviours. List 4-6 specific things a person would actually do. “Communication” becomes: summarise for an executive, give hard feedback, run a room, listen under pressure, write so people act.
  3. Pick the one that matters most this quarter. The behaviour that, if it improved first, would move something real.

The two-manager test

Could two managers watch the same person and agree on whether they have it?

“Communication” fails instantly. “Can summarise a complex update into three sentences an executive can act on” passes. If it passes the test, it is teachable, measurable, and provable. If it fails, you are about to spend a budget on a word.
