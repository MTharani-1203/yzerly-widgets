The One-Pass Email Checklist

The clearest writers are not the most eloquent. They are the ones whose message lands the first time, so nobody has to reply to ask what they meant. Here is the checklist that turns a four-reply thread into one.

Before you send

  1. Lead with the ask. Put what you need, and by when, in the first line.
  2. Make the decision explicit. State what you have decided or what you need decided, not just context.
  3. One purpose per message. Decision, input, or information. Not all three at once.

The clarity checks

  • Could a busy person act on this without replying to ask a question? If not, rewrite.
  • Is every name that needs to do something told exactly what?
  • Have you removed the context that does not change the action?

Why one pass matters

Every extra reply cycle multiplies across every person on the thread, every day. Clear writing is not a nicety; it is one of the cheapest pieces of time you can recover across an organisation. It is also a trainable skill, not a talent.
