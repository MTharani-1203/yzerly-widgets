The Clean Handover Checklist

Most delay and rework does not happen inside teams. It happens in the gap between them, where context is lost in translation. A handover is a communication act; here is how to make it a clean one.

Every handover carries five things

  1. A named owner on both sides, not a shared inbox.
  2. The why, not only the what.
  3. The open questions, travelling with the work.
  4. A confirm-to-act step: the receiver says what they will do, not just that they got it.
  5. The context that lived in one head, written down before it moves.

Design the seam, not just the teams

  • Standardise the format so nothing critical is left to assumption.
  • Measure where work stalls between teams, not only within them.
  • Trace rework back to the handover that caused it, and fix the seam.

Make it safe, not blaming

A dropped handover is a process failure, not an individual's. Treat it that way and people surface the breaks instead of hiding them. That is how seams get stronger over time rather than quietly leaking.
