The Adoption Loop: Getting Past Sixty Per Cent

Upskilling programmes rarely stall because the content is bad. They stall because there is no loop that turns a finished module into a changed behaviour. Here is the loop that pushes adoption past the plateau.

Find where it stalls

  • Start but not finish: no protected time, no manager expectation.
  • Finish but not apply: no bridge from module to real work.
  • Plateau after early adopters: no social proof or peer practice.
  • Works then fades: no reinforcement past the first month.

Close the loop

  1. Protect the time and put the manager in the loop, so learning does not lose to the day job.
  2. Attach an application task on real work within days of finishing.
  3. Make wins visible so adoption spreads socially, not by mandate.
  4. Add spaced reinforcement past thirty days so the behaviour holds.

Measure behaviour, not completion

Completion at sixty per cent measures who watched. Adoption measures who changed. Track whether the behaviour is still present at ninety days; that is the number that predicts whether the programme paid back.
