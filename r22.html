The Manager Micro-Behaviours That Compound

Your best manager's edge is rarely a talent. It is a handful of small communication habits, repeated, that never made it into any job description. Named and practised, they are teachable.

The five that matter most

  • Read-back before handoff: can you tell me your approach in your own words?
  • Quiet person first: ask the quietest capable voice before consensus sets.
  • Feedback in the moment: one specific, timely observation, not a saved-up rating.
  • Standing pre-mortem: if this misses, where will it miss, and what do you need now?
  • Growth check-in: when did you last feel stretched, and when did that stop?

Why small beats grand

These take minutes, not workshops. They work because they attach to a real, recent moment, which is the only place adults reliably learn. A manager who does five of these weekly outperforms one who runs an annual review flawlessly.

How to build them

  1. Pick one that matches your team's current symptom.
  2. Script the first line so it is ready under pressure.
  3. Do it for two weeks until it stops feeling awkward, then add the next.
