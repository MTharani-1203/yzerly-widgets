Escalation Triage: Conversation-Born vs Genuinely Complex

Not every escalation is trainable. This quick triage splits the ones a better first conversation would have prevented from the ones that are genuinely hard, so you spend where it moves the number.

Run this on your last 20 escalations

For each, mark yes/no:

  • Could a clearer first response have resolved it on contact?
  • Did the customer repeat themselves or get transferred before it escalated?
  • Was expectation-setting (timelines, next steps) missing or vague early on?
  • Was the issue itself routine, but the handling made it worse?

Three or four “yes” answers means conversation-born: trainable, and the highest-leverage place to cut cost. Zero or one means genuinely complex: a process, policy, or product fix, not a training one.

What to do with the split

  • Conversation-born pile: build the specific first-contact behaviours (listening, clarifying, expectation-setting) and measure escalation rate before and after.
  • Complex pile: route to product, policy, or process. Training will not move these, and pretending it will wastes the budget.
