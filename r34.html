The Compliance Conversation Playbook (BFSI)

In regulated selling, the rule is only as strong as the conversation that delivers it. Mis-selling is usually a communication failure, not a knowledge one. This playbook trains the conversation itself.

Disclose so it is understood

  • Explain the rule in plain language the customer can restate.
  • Say why the rule exists, not just that it does.
  • Check understanding, do not just confirm you disclosed.

Say no without losing the relationship

  1. Be clear, not vague, when the answer has to be no.
  2. Give the reason in the customer's terms.
  3. Offer the compliant path, so no is a redirection, not a wall.

Practise the hard moments

The pushy customer and the difficult disclosure should be rehearsed, not met for the first time live. Treat mis-selling risk as a communication risk, and train the conversation the way you train the product. That is where the licence and the customer are both protected.
