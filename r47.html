What Your Attrition Data Hides About Managers

The company attrition rate is calm enough to hide a manager quietly losing their best people. The story you need is at the manager level, over time. This guide shows how to read it.

Read it by manager, not in aggregate

  • Cluster exits by manager, and watch for patterns.
  • Separate regretted attrition from healthy turnover.
  • Track exit reasons by manager, over time, not as one-offs.

Do not let output hide the cost

  1. A high-output manager who burns people out is still a problem.
  2. Act on the pattern early, not after several exits.
  3. Support the manager whose attrition signals a fixable gap.

Attrition as a management signal

Some turnover is market and some is management. Treating all of it as market lets a manager-shaped problem hide in a company-shaped number. Reading attrition by manager turns a lagging statistic into an early, actionable signal.
