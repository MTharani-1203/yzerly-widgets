Busy or Productive? A Meeting Quality Guide

A full calendar feels like progress and often is not. The difference between a busy meeting and a productive one is a few communication disciplines. This guide makes meetings earn their cost.

What a productive meeting has

  • A clear purpose, stated up front.
  • A decision or output, not just discussion.
  • Only the people who need to be there.

The disciplines that get you there

  1. Pre-reads replace reading in the room.
  2. Status in writing, so the meeting is for the hard part.
  3. The point in three sentences, then stop.
  4. Actions and owners clear by the end.

Judge them honestly

Measure meetings by the decisions they produce, not by whether people enjoyed them, and be willing to cancel one that has no purpose this week. Productive meetings are a communication skill, and they buy back some of the most expensive time you have.
