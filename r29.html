The Discovery Call Question Bank

A great discovery call is mostly the buyer talking. The rep's skill is in the questions and the silences. This bank gives you the questions that surface cost, pressure, and the real decision, before anyone pitches anything.

Questions that surface the real problem

Cost: What is this problem costing you today, in time, money, or risk?
Pressure: Why is this on your desk now, rather than last year or next?
Inaction: What happens if you do nothing about it?

Questions that surface the real decision

  • Who else has to say yes for this to happen?
  • How will you measure whether it worked?
  • What would make you decide not to move forward?

The skills underneath

  1. Let silence work. After a real question, wait. The buyer fills it with the truth.
  2. Restate to confirm. Leave able to say the problem back in their words.
  3. Earn the next step. Agree an action the buyer owns, not a vague follow-up.
