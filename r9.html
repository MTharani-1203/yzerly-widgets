The Discovery Framework: Questions That Qualify and Shape

Deals are won or lost in the first real conversation. This framework turns the eight markers of strong discovery into a call you can run and coach.

Run the call in this order

  1. Problem, not product. “Walk me through what is actually happening today.” Explore at least three distinct problems before you say a word about your solution.
  2. Quantify. “What is that costing you, in time, money, or risk?”
  3. Consequence. “What happens if this stays as it is for another year?”
  4. Decision. “Who else needs to be comfortable for this to move?”
  5. Timing. “What is driving the timeline, and what happens if it slips?”
  6. Next step. Leave with a clear, dated commitment, not “we’ll be in touch.”
The whole time: listen more than you talk. Aim for the customer speaking most of the call. If you are demoing, you have skipped discovery.

The coaching move

Record calls, score them against these six, and coach the one weakest step. Win rate on the same pipeline is the number that proves it worked.
