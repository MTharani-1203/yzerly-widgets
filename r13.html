The New-Manager Onboarding Checklist

Most first-time managers are promoted for strong individual work, then left to figure out leadership alone. This checklist is what to put in place before and just after the promotion, so a good contributor becomes a good manager.

Before they get a team

  • Train feedback and difficult conversations before the role, not after the first crisis
  • Make the expectations of the manager role explicit, in writing
  • Assign a manager or mentor for the transition
  • Teach the shift from doing the work to enabling it

First 90 days

  • Set up a real one-on-one cadence, and coach them on running it
  • Walk them through their first delegation and their first performance conversation
  • Check the engagement of their team, not just their output
  • Give them a safe place to ask the questions they will not ask their own boss

The signal to watch

Regretted attrition and quiet disengagement often start one level down, under a brand-new manager who was never taught to lead. If exits or low engagement cluster under first-year managers, the fix is upstream: build the manager, before the team pays for the gap.
