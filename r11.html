The Completion-to-Capability Scorecard

Completion tells you people showed up. Capability tells you the work changed. This one-page scorecard is what to measure instead of completion, so leadership sees impact, not activity.

The four numbers that actually prove capability

  1. Behaviour change. The specific on-the-job behaviour the programme was meant to shift, observed after training, not attendance.
  2. Business-metric delta. Movement in a number you already report (CSAT, escalation rate, TAT, first-contact resolution, defect leakage), measured before and after.
  3. Manager confirmation. The people who see daily work confirming the change, not a feedback form.
  4. Capability by role. Where the skill sits for each role, not one blended average that hides the gaps.

How to run the scorecard

  • Baseline first. Capture the behaviour and the business metric before the programme runs. Without a baseline there is no proof.
  • Name the target behaviour. Write the exact behaviour a manager would see if the training worked. If you cannot write it, you cannot measure it.
  • Pick one business metric per programme. Tie each intervention to a single number leadership already tracks.
  • Re-measure at 30 and 90 days. Decay is real. A one-time post-test overstates the result.

What to stop reporting

Completion rate, attendance, and average satisfaction are inputs, not outcomes. Keep them for operations if you must, but never present them to leadership as proof that capability improved. Lead every readout with the four numbers above.
