Earning the Seat: A GCC Influence Guide

A capability centre does not earn a seat at the table by delivering more work. It earns it by influence, by the parent trusting its judgement. This guide maps the communication that moves a centre up the value curve.

What actually earns influence

  • A point of view offered before it is asked for, on things the centre can see and the parent cannot.
  • Outcomes in the parent's language, not utilisation and seats.
  • Local leaders who influence the business, not just route work.

The moves that shift perception

  1. Propose the next work, scoped and risk-named, before it is handed down.
  2. Bring a problem the parent has not seen yet, with your read on it.
  3. Put local leaders in front of the business on real decisions, and back them.

The traps that keep a centre downstream

Waiting to be invited, reporting only cost and utilisation, and keeping leadership as delivery management all keep a centre in execution. The capabilities that move it up, communication, influence, judgement, are exactly the ones the cost-centre budget never funds. Build them on purpose.
