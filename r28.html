The Clean Sales-to-Delivery Handover

A deal is won on a promise and a relationship. Too often it is handed to a delivery team that receives only a signed scope. This is how to move a client across the seam without dropping context, promises, or trust.

What must cross the seam

  1. The why. What the client is really buying, and the pressure behind it.
  2. The promises. Everything sales committed to, written down, not held in a rep's head.
  3. The people. A warm introduction, so the client does not re-explain from scratch.
  4. The owner. A named handover owner on both sides.

Design the handover as a ritual

  • Sales and delivery meet on the account before kick-off, not after a problem.
  • Context travels in a standard format, not a forwarded email chain.
  • Delivery flags any promise it cannot keep before the client feels it as a miss.

Why it protects revenue

The gap between the promise that closed the deal and the experience that follows is where good accounts sour early. A clean handover is one of the cheapest ways to protect renewal and referral, and it is a communication discipline, not a CRM field.
