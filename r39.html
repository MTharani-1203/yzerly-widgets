Measuring Soft Skills Without Vanity Metrics

You can measure a soft skill. You just cannot do it with a satisfaction score. The trick is to observe behaviour and move a business metric, with a real before and after. This guide shows how, skill by skill.

The method, in three steps

  1. Pick an observable behaviour the skill should change.
  2. Baseline it from real work before you start.
  3. Re-measure the same thing after, and attribute honestly.

Honest metrics, by skill

Feedback: the share of people who can name a specific, recent piece of it.
Writing: one-pass rate, the share of messages needing no clarifying reply.
Discovery: the share of calls that reach cost, pressure, and the real decision.

The traps to avoid

  • Satisfaction scores measure how it felt, not whether behaviour changed.
  • Knowledge quizzes measure what people know, not what they do.
  • Lagging metrics alone are too noisy and slow to attribute; pair them with a behaviour.
