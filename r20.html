The CFO Conversation Script

A CFO does not cut L&D because it has no value. They cut it because it has no number. This script turns any programme into the language finance already speaks: baseline, attribution, delta.

Before the conversation

  1. Pick one business metric the CFO already tracks that your programme should move.
  2. Baseline it from existing data before you spend a rupee on training.
  3. Estimate the value of a small move, one week of ramp, one avoided exit, one win-rate point.

The four sentences that land

1. Here is the number we want to move, and where it sits today.
2. Here is what a modest move is worth in rupees, with the maths.
3. Here is how we will attribute the change to this work, not to noise.
4. Here is the date we re-measure and report back.

What to stop saying

  • Completion rates, hours delivered, and headcount trained. These measure activity, not value.
  • Satisfaction and confidence scores. Useful internally, invisible to a CFO.
  • Anchor on levels 3 and 4 of the Kirkpatrick model, behaviour and results, not levels 1 and 2.
