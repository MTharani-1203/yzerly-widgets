The Skip-Level Playbook

A skip-level is a controlled experiment in whether truth can travel upward. These questions are non-leading by design, so the honest answer is easier to give than the polite one.

Design rules

  • Ask for the last time something happened, not whether it happens.
  • Ask what they would change if it were their call.
  • Ask what the manager does well first, then what they wish were different.
  • Follow every safe answer with: can you give me a recent example?

Question bank

Retention: When did you last feel genuinely stretched and growing here? And when did that stop?
Trust: When you raise a concern, what usually happens to it? Walk me through the last time.
Workload: What work could stop tomorrow with no real consequence? What stops us from stopping it?

Reading the answers

  • Strong answers are concrete: real examples, named trade-offs, clear asks.
  • Evasive answers are abstract and reassuring: everyone is great, no complaints, all good.
  • The gap between the two is your signal. Track it by manager, over time.
