Psychological Safety Is a Set of Behaviours

Psychological safety is not a mood, a perk, or a poster on the wall. It is built, or eroded, by specific things managers do in specific moments. This guide names the behaviours so you can build it on purpose.

What managers actually do

  • Invite challenge, and thank the person who disagrees.
  • Admit their own mistakes openly, first.
  • Respond to concerns by acting, not defending.

What it produces

  1. Bad news arrives early, while it is still cheap to fix.
  2. People ask for help and say I do not know, without cost.
  3. The quietest voices get heard, by design, not by luck.

Why behaviours beat slogans

A values statement about safety changes nothing; a manager who reliably rewards candour changes everything. Because it is behavioural, it is teachable, and because it is teachable, safety is something you can build rather than hope for.
