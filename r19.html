The First 90 Days Communication Plan

New joiners rarely fail on the work. They fail on the unspoken: how decisions get made, what good looks like, and whether it is safe to ask. This plan makes the unspoken explicit, week by week.

Week 1: make it safe to ask

  • Name a go-to person. One human whose job is to answer basic questions without judgement.
  • Explain how decisions really get made, not just the org chart.
  • Hold a real one-to-one, not only a welcome chat. Ask what is already confusing.

Weeks 2 to 4: define good and give feedback

  • Show what good looks like for the role, with a real example.
  • Give early, specific feedback on real work. Do not wait for the review.
  • Check they can explain the work back, not just complete it.
  • Point out which meetings matter and how to read them.

Weeks 5 to 12: catch the quiet early

  • Watch for the week-three silence. When a new joiner goes quiet, act; do not wait.
  • Ask what is still unclear while it is fresh, and fix the cause.
  • Spot a struggling joiner before probation, not at it. The review should hold no surprises.
