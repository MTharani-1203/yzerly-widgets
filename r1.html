The 3 Exit Questions That Surface the Real Reason People Leave

Exit interviews default to “pay” because it is the safe answer. These three questions get underneath it. Ask them before the notice period ends, ideally in a skip-level, and resist the urge to defend.

The three questions

1. “Think about your best day here in the last six months. What were you doing, and when did days like that stop?”
2. “Was there a specific moment you decided to start looking? What happened just before it?”
3. “If one thing had been different, and it can’t be money, what would have kept you?”

How to run it

  • Ask early. Once someone has mentally left, candour drops. The first week after resignation is your window.
  • Have someone other than the direct manager ask. People will not name the manager to the manager.
  • Do not defend or explain. The moment you justify, the honesty closes. Just listen and note the pattern.
  • Track answers by manager. The signal is in the clustering, not the individual exit.

What you are listening for

Stalled growth, feedback that never came, a manager who never asked what they wanted. If two or three exits under the same manager rhyme, you have found a conversation problem wearing a pay costume.
