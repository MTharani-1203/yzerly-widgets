The Coordination Playbook: Why Projects Slip

Cross-functional projects rarely miss dates because a team ran out of capacity. They miss because of coordination drag: waiting, re-deciding, and dropped handoffs between teams. This playbook cuts the drag.

Design the seams between teams

  • Give every dependency a named owner on both sides.
  • Keep one shared plan, not separate views.
  • Make cross-team decisions once, and do not re-open them.

Surface the drag early

  1. Flag a slipping dependency early, not at the deadline.
  2. Measure where work waits between teams, not only within them.
  3. Trace a missed date to the coordination break, not to effort.

Give coordination real teeth

A coordinator with no authority cannot hold a date. Cross-functional roles need real decision rights, and status should travel in writing so meetings are reserved for the genuinely hard parts. Coordination is a communication discipline, and it is where the schedule is won.
